Geography

The city is located along the shore of the Great Sea in the Northwestern region of Elwynn Forest of the Eastern Kingdom continent of Azeroth and is divided up into 7 distinct geographic regions. • Cathedral Square - The most affluent. The primary religious center of the kingdom. While the Cathedral itself located in the district, it does not fall within the confines of the laws of Stormwind and operates autonomously from the government. • Dwarven District - The Dwarfs of Ironforge have established an enclave known as the foremost industrial center of the kingdom. The majority of the kingdom's blacksmiths, forgers, and like-minded professions reside within the district. • Harbor - A major hub of economic development and activity for not only the city, but for the kingdom as a whole. • Mage Quarter - Included is “Lion's Rest”, a peaceful place to commemorate the late King Varian Wrynn. Also, a place of study for mages to train to increase their knowledge and powers. Several portals to other locations within the kingdom are located within the tower. The Stockades, where criminals arrested by the City Guard are housed is also located in the quarter. • Old Town - Many buildings and parts predate the reconstruction of the city and it's razing by the Orcs during the events of the First War. Crime is a constant problem due to the run-down, rustic, downtrodden nature of the area where most of the poor folk of the city reside. • Trade District - Serving as the economic hub of the capital, the district houses more businesses than other districts in the kingdom and generates more tax revenue than any other district. Though not many people live in the district, those that do tend to be of noble affluence and live in relativity large houses. • Unincorporated - Includes area within the city that are not otherwise assigned to a different region, such as the Outskirts and the area around the Stormwind Embassy.
